Nightcue

Privacy policy

Last updated: 2026-05-29

Nightcue is a paced ear-prompter for Android. It reads any text you paste into it aloud, one line at a time, using your device's built-in text-to-speech engine. The app runs entirely on your device.

This page explains, in plain language, what information Nightcue handles — and what it doesn't.

Data we collect

None. Nightcue does not collect, transmit, or share any personal information. There are no accounts, no analytics, no advertising, no usage telemetry, and no tracking of any kind.

Data stored on your device

The following information is stored only in Nightcue's private storage on your device. None of it is uploaded to a server, sent over the network, or shared with us or any third party:

  • The text you paste into the app (stories, speeches, scripture, song lyrics, or any other passage you choose to read aloud).
  • Your pacing, voice, and playback preferences.
  • The last unit you played, so the app can offer to resume where you left off.
  • Your saved-session library (titles, settings, and content for each saved entry).
  • A small set of one-shot flags (e.g. "first-launch hint already shown") so the app doesn't repeat onboarding prompts.

Uninstalling Nightcue removes all of this. There is no server-side data to delete; the on-device data is the data, and Android's app-private storage model gives you full control over it.

Permissions and why we use them

  • Internet — required by the Capacitor framework that Nightcue is built on. The app itself makes no network requests.
  • Foreground service, foreground service (media playback), wake lock, ignore battery optimizations — used solely to keep text-to-speech playback running while your screen is off, so a reading or rehearsal session isn't cut short when the phone locks.
  • Post notifications — used to display the playback notification that Android requires for any foreground media service. The notification also surfaces play/pause/next/previous controls on your lock screen.
  • Billing (Google Play) — required to offer the optional one-time "Nightcue Pro" purchase. See the next section.

The microphone is never accessed. Nightcue does not request the RECORD_AUDIO permission, and no microphone code ships in the app.

In-app purchases (Nightcue Pro)

Nightcue offers a single one-time purchase called Nightcue Pro, which unlocks an unlimited saved-session library. The free version of the app remains fully functional and is capped at three saved sessions.

How the purchase is handled. When you tap Unlock Pro, the transaction is processed by Google Play Billing, a service operated by Google. Nightcue does not see or store your name, address, payment-card details, or any other billing information — those are handled entirely by Google under Google's own privacy policy.

The only thing Nightcue stores after a successful purchase is a boolean flag, kept in app-private storage, indicating that Pro is unlocked on this device. This flag is reconciled against Google Play's purchase ledger each time the app launches, so refunds and family- sharing changes propagate without us tracking anything ourselves.

Third parties

Nightcue does not contain advertising SDKs, analytics SDKs, crash reporters, or any other third-party data-collection libraries. The only external services the app interacts with are:

  • Your device's text-to-speech engine (typically Google's Speech Services or Samsung's TTS, depending on your device). This is part of the Android operating system and runs locally. The text you paste is read aloud by that engine on-device; it is not sent to a cloud service by Nightcue. If your device's TTS engine itself uses a network connection for higher-quality voices, that behavior is governed by the engine provider's own privacy policy and is outside Nightcue's control.
  • Google Play Billing, as described above, only when you tap Unlock Pro or restore a previous purchase.

Children

Nightcue is intended for use by adults. The app is not directed at children, is not in Google Play's Designed for Families program, and does not knowingly collect any information from children. Because the app collects no information from anyone, the children's-privacy promise is the same as the general one — nothing is collected.

Changes to this policy

If Nightcue's data-handling practices ever change in a material way, the updated policy will be published at this URL and the "Last updated" date above will be bumped accordingly. Where the change affects the answers given in the Google Play Data Safety form, that form will be updated to match.
